Understanding Different Types of Stinging Insects in Mount Prospect

Identifying the specific type of stinging insect you're dealing with is crucial for effective and safe removal. Different species have different behaviors, nesting habits, and levels of aggression. Our team is trained to accurately identify common species found in Mount Prospect, IL.

Yellow Jackets

Yellow jackets are a type of social wasp known for their aggressive nature, especially when their nests are disturbed. They are often mistakenly called 'bees,' but they are wasps. They are typically bright yellow and black with a slender waist. Yellow jacket nests are usually located in the ground, in wall voids, or in other protected cavities. They are attracted to sweets and proteins, making them common nuisances at picnics and outdoor gatherings. Their stings can be painful and, in some individuals, can trigger allergic reactions. Due to their aggressive nature, yellow jacket nest removal should always be handled by professionals. Attempting to remove a ground nest without proper protection can lead to a swarm attack, which can be incredibly dangerous. They are often mistaken for honey bees due to their yellow and black coloring, but their behavior and body shape are distinctly different. Yellow jackets are predatory and will attack when their nest is threatened. Their nests, made of a papery material, can grow quite large throughout the summer months. They are a significant source of stinging incidents in the late summer and fall when their colonies are at their largest and they are actively foraging for food sources before winter. Dealing with yellow jackets requires specialized knowledge and equipment to ensure the safety of everyone involved. Our technicians are experienced in locating and safely eliminating yellow jacket nests, whether they are in the ground, in walls, or other difficult-to-reach locations. We understand the risks associated with these insects and take every precaution to perform the removal safely and effectively, minimizing the potential for stings. Paper Wasps

Paper wasps are another common type of social wasp in the Mount Prospect area. They are typically brown or reddish-brown with yellow markings and have a characteristic pinched waist. Paper wasps construct umbrella-shaped nests made of a papery material, often found hanging from eaves, porch ceilings, railings, and tree branches. While generally less aggressive than yellow jackets, paper wasps will sting if their nest is disturbed or if they feel threatened. Their stings can be painful. Paper wasp nests are often visible and relatively easy to locate compared to yellow jacket nests hidden underground or within structures. However, removing a paper wasp nest still carries a risk of stings, especially from a larger or more established colony. It's advisable to have a professional remove paper wasp nests, particularly those located in high-traffic areas or near entryways. Professionals can safely approach and treat the nest, ensuring all the wasps are eliminated and the nest is removed without incident. Identifying the species of wasp is important because treatment methods can vary. Paper wasps are less likely to forage for sweets and are more focused on collecting insects and wood fibers for their nests. They are often seen building their nests in sheltered locations to protect them from the elements. While a small paper wasp nest might seem manageable, a larger colony can contain hundreds of wasps, all capable of stinging defensively. Hornets

Hornets are the largest of the social wasps and can be quite intimidating. The European hornet, an introduced species, and the bald-faced hornet (which is actually a type of yellow jacket) are found in Illinois. Hornets build large, football-shaped paper nests, often hanging from trees, shrubs, or structures. They are known for their painful stings and can be aggressive, particularly if their nest is threatened. Bald-faced hornets, despite their name, are black and white and are quite aggressive when it comes to defending their nests. European hornets are larger and more reddish-brown or yellowish-brown. Due to their size and potentially aggressive nature, hornet nest removal is a high-risk task and should only be performed by trained professionals wearing appropriate protective gear. Hornet nests can grow significantly in size throughout the summer, housing thousands of individuals. The larger the nest, the higher the risk and complexity of removal. Attempting to remove a hornet nest yourself can result in a massive defensive attack, leading to multiple painful stings, which can be dangerous, especially for individuals with allergies. Our technicians are equipped and trained to safely and effectively remove hornet nests from various locations, regardless of size or accessibility.
